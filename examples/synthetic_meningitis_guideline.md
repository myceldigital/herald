# Synthetic Acute Bacterial Meningitis Guideline

> This is a fully synthetic demonstration guideline created for Herald examples and tests. It is not clinical advice.

## 1. Scope

This synthetic guideline covers empiric antimicrobial therapy for suspected acute bacterial meningitis in adults. The goal is to demonstrate how Herald captures urgent recommendations, branching logic, contraindications, and traceable source text.

## 2. Recommendations

### 2.1 Adult empiric therapy

Administer ceftriaxone plus vancomycin immediately when acute bacterial meningitis is suspected in adults.

Blood cultures should be obtained immediately, but antibiotic administration must not be delayed for culture acquisition or imaging.

### 2.2 Listeria coverage

In adults aged 50 years or older, add ampicillin to the empiric regimen to cover Listeria.

In immunocompromised adults, add ampicillin to the empiric regimen because Listeria coverage is required.

### 2.3 Immediate beta-lactam allergy

If there is a history of immediate beta-lactam anaphylaxis, seek urgent specialist advice and use a non-beta-lactam empiric regimen.

### 2.4 Adjunctive corticosteroids

Give dexamethasone before or with the first dose of antibiotics when bacterial meningitis is strongly suspected.
